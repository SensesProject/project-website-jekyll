What is the vision?

The SENSES project investigates potential socio-economic futures in the face of climate change and how this knowledge can be made accessible to a broader public (www.senses-project.org).

SENSES will develop tools and approaches to make the new generation of climate change scenarios more comprehensible, culminating in a climate service for decision makers. We will identify central needs for this step in a co-creation process between scientists and decision makers from policy and business. And here is why we contact you.

Why should you participate?

In times of high volatility and complexity, the new generation of climate- change scenarios can be a helpful means to investigate potential futures, understanding risks and opportunities.

They allow to take an integrative look on climate change and its impacts (e.g. floods, droughts, etc), but also on adaptation and mitigation - on how socio-economic factors (e.g. population, health, energy, finance, transport, â€¦) factors can change in the face of global warming. Decision makers can benefit substantially from having access to this data.

How can you participate?

If you participate in our survey (~ duration: 15m) and you leave your contact details you will be part of our pool of stakeholders. This means you will

  • get a primer on scenarios, helping you to understand how they can be used
  • be invited to test prototypes
  • potentially be invited to our interactive workshops
  • have direct influence on the shape of the final product
  • be kept informed about latest developments via our newsletter (one email per quarter)

Who are we looking for?

If you are a national or international climate policy maker, a regional climate scenario user, or are concerned about how future climate change will impact your business, we would like to work with you to identify your information needs in the context of climate change scenarios.

For example, the Climate Scenario Toolkit can help to inform climate policy makers about the global implications of national contributions under the Paris Agreement and business leaders about long-term investment opportunities and risks related to climate change and climate policies. On a regional level, scenarios can be used to explore inter-linkages with other sustainable development goals, e.g. relating to food, water and energy availability and poverty.

Who are we?

The project consortium has internationally recognized expertise in climate change scenario research and design and visualization techniques, and large experience in working with the three user groups targeted in this project.
